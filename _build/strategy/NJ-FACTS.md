## VERIFIED NEW JERSEY TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **Gross Income Tax (GIT) — GRADUATED, not flat.** Rates run from a low of **1.4%** up to a top marginal rate of
  **10.75% on taxable income over $1,000,000** (the "millionaire's tax," in place since 2020), with several brackets
  in between. **Do NOT quote the middle brackets as fixed figures** unless added here — frame the schedule
  qualitatively ("graduated from 1.4% to 10.75%") and link the official rate schedules. This is a key contrast with
  the flat-tax states (PA 3.07%, IN 2.95%). Source: NJ Division of Taxation — Income Tax Rates / Tax Rate Schedules
  (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/taxtables.shtml`).
- **PA–NJ Reciprocal Income Tax Agreement — the genuinely distinct cross-border angle (ties to the PA anchor).**
  Compensation paid to a **Pennsylvania resident working in New Jersey is NOT subject to NJ income tax** (and a NJ
  resident working in PA is not subject to PA income tax). The employee files **Form NJ-165 (Employee's Certificate
  of Nonresidence in New Jersey)** with the employer, who then **stops withholding NJ income tax** and withholds for
  the residence state instead. Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - Covers **wages/compensation only** (salaries, wages, tips, fees, commissions, bonuses).
  - **Does NOT cover local/municipal taxes** — e.g. the **Philadelphia Wage Tax** still applies to a Philadelphia
    resident, and is not waived by the reciprocal agreement.
  - QuickBooks Payroll has to be configured per employee so a PA-resident NJ worker (with an NJ-165 on file) has NJ
    withholding turned off and PA withholding on.
  - Source: NJ Division of Taxation — PA/NJ Reciprocal Income Tax Agreement (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/njit25.shtml`);
    Form NJ-165.
- **The NJ payroll-contribution stack — the genuine QuickBooks Payroll complexity (analog of PA Act 32 / IN county LIT).**
  Beyond GIT withholding, New Jersey runs a multi-fund payroll system with **both employer and employee**
  contributions across **two wage bases** (2026, VERIFIED):
  - **Two 2026 taxable wage bases:** **$44,800** (employer UI, TDI, WF/SWF; and employee UI, WF/SWF) and
    **$171,100** (employee TDI and FLI).
  - **Unemployment Insurance (UI/SUI):** new employers pay a combined **2.8%** (UI + WF/SWF) on the first $44,800 for
    the first three years; established employers are **experience-rated** (employer UI varies by reserve ratio).
  - **Temporary Disability Insurance (TDI/SDI):** employer contribution is **experience-rated**; the **employee TDI
    rate for 2026 is 0.19%** (down from 0.23%) on wages up to $171,100.
  - **Family Leave Insurance (FLI):** **no employer contribution**; the **employee FLI rate for 2026 is 0.23%** (down
    from 0.33%) on wages up to $171,100.
  - **Workforce Development / Supplemental Workforce (WF/SWF):** small employer + employee contributions on the
    $44,800 base, bundled with UI.
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** the specific 2026 employee rates (TDI 0.19%, FLI 0.23%) and wage bases are stated above;
    employer UI/TDI rates are **experience-rated and vary by employer**, so frame them qualitatively and link NJ DOL.
    Source: NJ Dept of Labor & Workforce Development — 2026 benefit/contribution rates (`nj.gov/labor`, Dec 29 2025
    announcement); NJ Division of Employer Accounts rate information.
- **Corporation Business Tax (CBT)** — **9%** on entire net income over $100,000; **7.5%** for income $50,001–$100,000;
  **6.5%** for income of $50,000 or less. Source: NJ Division of Taxation — CBT Overview (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/corp_over.shtml`).
- **Corporate Transit Fee (CTF)** — a **2.5% surtax** for privilege periods **Jan 1, 2024 through Dec 31, 2028** on
  CBT taxpayers with **taxable net income over $10,000,000**, **in addition to** the CBT (so the largest C-corps face
  a combined top rate of roughly **11.5%**). **No tax credits** may offset it; it **does not apply to NJ S
  corporations or public utilities**; revenue funds NJ Transit. Source: NJ Division of Taxation — Corporate Transit
  Fee (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/cbt/corporatetransitfee.shtml`).
- **Sales & Use Tax — 6.625% statewide**, with **no general local sales tax**. Two reduced-rate exceptions at exactly
  **half the state rate (3.3125%)**: qualifying sales by certified businesses in an **Urban Enterprise Zone (UEZ)** and
  certain sales by businesses located in **Salem County** (the two benefits do not stack — one 3.3125% rate, not lower).
  Source: NJ Division of Taxation — Sales Tax / UEZ & Salem County (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/businesses/salestax/`).
- **Pass-Through Business Alternative Income Tax (PTE/BAIT) — the distinct advisory angle.** NJ offers an **elective
  entity-level tax** on pass-through entities (partnerships, S corps, multi-member LLCs) that lets the business deduct
  the NJ tax **federally** as a SALT-cap workaround; members receive a **refundable NJ credit** for their share. The
  election and the credit interaction are genuinely NJ-distinct and a real reason a growing NJ pass-through wants
  advisory coordination. Frame as an advisory/coordination item (TechBrot keeps the books BAIT-ready; the CPA makes
  the election and files). Source: NJ Division of Taxation — Pass-Through Business Alternative Income Tax
  (`nj.gov/treasury/taxation/businesses/pte/`).
- **Local employer payroll taxes (Newark & Jersey City) — EMPLOYER-side, not employee withholding.** **Newark** levies
  a **payroll tax (about 1%)** on employer payroll for employers above a size threshold; **Jersey City** levies a
  **1% payroll tax** on the wages of employees who are **not Jersey City residents**. These are **employer-paid** taxes
  (distinct from NJ has-no-general-local-income-tax). Frame qualitatively (rates/thresholds change; employer-side) and
  link the respective city. Note these are separate from, and not waived by, the PA–NJ reciprocal agreement.
