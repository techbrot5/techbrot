## VERIFIED TENNESSEE TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **NO state individual income tax — the headline draw.** Tennessee levies **no broad individual income tax** on wages,
  salaries, or most personal income. The old **Hall income tax** (which applied only to certain interest and dividend
  income, never to wages) was **fully repealed for tax years beginning January 1, 2021**. Frame: **"Tennessee has no
  individual income tax &mdash; the Hall tax on investment income was fully repealed in 2021."** Source: Tennessee Dept of
  Revenue — Hall Income Tax; HIT-4 Hall Income Tax Rate.
- **No state income-tax withholding for employers — a clean payroll contrast.** Because there is no individual income tax,
  Tennessee employers **do not withhold state income tax** from wages (payroll is federal + FICA + state unemployment
  only). A genuine simplification vs income-tax states. Source: Tennessee Dept of Revenue — GEN-34 Income Tax Withholding.
- **Franchise & Excise (F&E) tax — the REAL business tax; the hook.** Tennessee's two-part business-level tax, filed
  together (FAE 170) by entities chartered, qualified, or doing business in TN (corporations, LPs, LLCs, business trusts):
  - **Excise tax — a flat 6.5%** of Tennessee net earnings (taxable income).
  - **Franchise tax — 0.25% of Tennessee net worth** ($0.25 per $100 of net worth), with a **$100 minimum** payable by any
    registered entity, active or inactive.
  - **2024 change (cite it):** for tax years ending on/after **January 1, 2024**, the old **property measure (the
    "minimum measure," Schedule G)** of the franchise tax was **repealed** — franchise tax is now computed on **net worth
    only (Schedule F)**. (A refund window for prior property-measure tax ran May 15–Nov 30, 2024 and is now closed.)
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** TechBrot keeps the books F&E-ready (clean net-worth and net-earnings figures) and coordinates with
    the client's CPA/EA; the **CPA/EA files the FAE 170**. Source: Tennessee Dept of Revenue — Franchise & Excise Tax; Due
    Dates and Tax Rates; FT-13 Property Measure Repeal.
- **Business tax (the local gross-receipts tax) — distinct from F&E; the second hook.** Separate from F&E, Tennessee levies
  a **business tax on gross receipts**, structured as **two taxes: a state business tax and a city business tax**. A
  business that **grosses $100,000 or more** in a county and/or incorporated municipality must **register and remit**. Rates
  are low and **vary by business classification**; it is a **gross-receipts** tax (on sales, not profit), administered by the
  Dept of Revenue but reaching both county and city levels. This is the **local business / gross-receipts** layer owners
  must track. Source: Tennessee Dept of Revenue — Business Tax; Gross Receipts Taxes.
- **High combined sales tax — among the highest in the country.** The **state general sales-and-use-tax rate is 7%** (one of
  the highest state rates in the US). Counties and cities add a **local option tax in multiples of 0.25%, up to a 2.75%
  maximum**, and **every Tennessee locality has a local rate** — so the **combined rate commonly lands around 9.25%–9.75%**,
  giving Tennessee one of the **highest average combined sales-tax rates in the nation**. (Groceries are taxed at a reduced
  state rate — keep the exact food rate qualitative and link the source.) Keep specific local rates **qualitative**; link the
  Dept of Revenue local-rate map. Source: Tennessee Dept of Revenue — Sales & Use Tax; Local Sales Tax.
- **Gross receipts taxes (privilege) — a narrow extra.** Tennessee also imposes **gross receipts taxes on certain specific
  activities** (e.g. utilities, bottlers) separate from the general business tax — mention only qualitatively if relevant;
  most small businesses won't owe these. Source: Tennessee Dept of Revenue — Gross Receipts Taxes.
