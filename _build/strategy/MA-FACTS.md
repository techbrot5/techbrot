## VERIFIED MASSACHUSETTS TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **5% flat income tax + the 4% "millionaires" surtax — THE MA-distinct hook.** Massachusetts taxes most classes of income
  at a **flat 5%**. On top of that, the **4% surtax** (the voter-enacted "Fair Share Amendment" / "millionaires tax,"
  effective for tax years beginning on/after Jan 1 2023) applies to the portion of **taxable income above an inflation-indexed
  threshold**: **$1,083,150 for tax year 2025** and **$1,107,750 for tax year 2026**. Only the income **above** the threshold
  is taxed at the extra 4% (so 9% total on that portion). This is the standout MA planning issue: it makes **owner
  compensation, distributions, business-sale gains, and timing** matter enormously near the threshold, and it drives
  entity/PTE planning. MA HAS an income tax, so **payroll carries Massachusetts state income-tax withholding** (Form M-4) — a
  contrast to WA/TN (no withholding), like CO. Frame the threshold tied to the year (it indexes annually). Source:
  Massachusetts DOR — Massachusetts 4% Surtax on Taxable Income; Massachusetts Tax Rates.
- **The corporate excise — MA's two-measure business tax.** A C-corporation pays the **corporate excise** as the GREATER of
  two measures combined: an **8% net-income measure** (8% of Massachusetts taxable net income, the current general-corporation
  rate) **PLUS** a **property-or-net-worth measure of $2.60 per $1,000** of Massachusetts taxable tangible property or net
  worth, with a **$456 minimum excise**. (Financial institutions and S-corps have their own rules.) Keep it framed as the
  general C-corp rule; the CPA computes and files. Source: Massachusetts DOR — Corporate Excise Tax Guide.
- **Flat 6.25% sales/use tax — NO local add-on (a clean contrast to CO/WA).** Massachusetts levies a **flat 6.25% state
  sales and use tax** statewide, with **no county or city local-option add-on** — so unlike Colorado's home-rule fragmentation
  or Washington's local layering, the rate is the same everywhere in the state. (Use tax mirrors it on untaxed out-of-state
  purchases.) The simplicity here is itself the story for multi-state sellers used to worse. Source: Massachusetts DOR — Sales
  and Use Tax.
- **MA Paid Family & Medical Leave (PFML) — the payroll contribution.** Massachusetts runs a state **PFML** program funded by
  a payroll contribution. For **2026 the total contribution is 0.88%** of eligible wages (family + medical leave), split
  between employer and employee: of the medical-leave portion, up to 40% may be withheld from the employee and the employer
  covers the rest; the family-leave portion may be fully withheld from the employee. **Employers with fewer than 25 covered
  individuals are not required to pay the employer share** (an effective 0.46% in that case). Self-employed individuals who opt
  in contribute 0.88%. Keep the rate tied to 2026 and link the source (it adjusts). Source: MA Dept of Family and Medical
  Leave — PFML employer contribution rates.
- **The PTE excise (entity-level election) — the advisory angle.** Massachusetts allows eligible pass-through entities (S-corps,
  partnerships) to **elect to pay an entity-level excise (at the 5% rate) on qualified income**, so owners can claim a federal
  deduction and a refundable MA credit (a SALT-cap workaround) — especially relevant now that the 4% surtax raises individual
  exposure. Advisory/coordination item — the CPA makes the election and files; TechBrot keeps the books PTE-ready. Source:
  Massachusetts DOR — pass-through entity / 63D entity-level tax.
