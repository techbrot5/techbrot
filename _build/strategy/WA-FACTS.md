## VERIFIED WASHINGTON TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **NO personal income tax AND no corporate income tax.** Washington levies **neither an individual nor a corporate income
  tax**. So there is **no state income-tax withholding** on payroll. But "no income tax" is NOT "low tax" &mdash; the state
  raises revenue through the **B&amp;O tax**, a **high combined sales/use tax**, and (since 2022) a **capital-gains tax**.
  Source: Washington Dept of Revenue &mdash; Income tax (`dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/income-tax`).
- **Business &amp; Occupation (B&amp;O) tax — the REAL business tax; the WA-distinct hook.** The B&amp;O is a **gross-receipts
  tax** on the value of products or gross income of the business, levied **by classification** (50+ classifications). The
  defining feature: it is on **gross receipts with NO deduction** for labor, materials, taxes, rent, or any other cost of
  doing business &mdash; so even a low-margin or break-even business owes it. Verified rates:
  - **Retailing — 0.471%**; **Wholesaling — 0.484%**; **Manufacturing — 0.484%** of gross receipts.
  - **Service &amp; Other Activities — TIERED (effective Oct 1, 2025):** **1.5%** for businesses with less than $1M of
    prior-year taxable income, **1.75%** for $1M to under $5M, and a **higher top tier for $5M and above** (confirm the exact
    top rate on the DOR classifications page &mdash; keep qualitative).
  - **City B&amp;O tax — the extra layer:** many Washington cities (Seattle, Tacoma, Bellevue, Everett, and others) levy their
    **own local B&amp;O tax** on top of the state B&amp;O, administered by the city (not DOR). Keep specific city B&amp;O rates
    **qualitative**; link the city. This is a genuine compliance burden generic out-of-state bookkeeping misses.
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** TechBrot configures QuickBooks to track gross receipts by B&amp;O classification (and by city where a
    local B&amp;O applies) and reconciles the figures; the **business or its CPA files the Combined Excise Tax Return**. Source:
    Washington Dept of Revenue &mdash; Business &amp; Occupation tax; B&amp;O tax classifications.
- **High combined sales/use tax — among the highest in the US.** The **state sales-tax rate is 6.5%**, and local
  jurisdictions add their own on top, so the **combined rate commonly lands around 8.5%&ndash;10.5%** by location (the Seattle
  metro is near the top in the nation). Washington is **destination-based** for sourcing. Keep specific combined local rates
  **qualitative**; link the DOR rate lookup. **Use tax** mirrors the sales tax on untaxed purchases (notably relevant near the
  **Oregon border**, since Oregon has no sales tax). Source: Washington Dept of Revenue &mdash; Sales &amp; use tax rates; Local
  sales &amp; use tax.
- **Capital-gains tax — the 2022 catch on a "no-income-tax" state (an individual excise tax).** Since the **2021 legislature
  (ESSB 5096, RCW 82.87)**, Washington imposes a **7% tax on long-term capital gains** above a **standard deduction**
  ($270,000 for 2024; **$278,000 for 2025**, inflation-indexed). **Tiered from tax year 2025:** 7% on the first $1M of taxable
  Washington capital gains above the deduction, plus an **additional 2.9%** on the excess over $1M (a 9.9% top combined rate).
  **Exempt:** real estate, retirement-account assets, assets used in a trade or business that qualify for federal depreciation
  or expensing, timber/timberland, livestock for farming, commercial fishing privileges, and certain franchised-auto-dealer
  goodwill. It is an **individual** excise tax (it applies to owners on the sale of stocks/bonds/business interests, not to the
  business&rsquo;s ordinary income). **HONEST FRAMING:** TechBrot keeps clean cost-basis and gain records so the owner&rsquo;s
  CPA can determine and file any capital-gains tax; **TechBrot does not file it**. Source: Washington Dept of Revenue &mdash;
  Capital gains tax; New tiered rates special notice.
- **Washington payroll premiums — the WA-distinct payroll stack (no income-tax withholding, but two premiums).** Because
  there is no income tax, employers withhold **no state income tax**, but Washington runs two payroll premiums:
  - **Paid Family &amp; Medical Leave (PFML):** the **2026 premium is 1.13%** of wages, split **employer 28.57% / employee
    71.43%**; **employers with fewer than 50 employees** are **not required to pay the employer share** (but still collect and
    remit the employee share). Source: WA Employment Security Department &mdash; PFML premium rate increases to 1.13% in 2026
    (`esd.wa.gov`).
  - **WA Cares Fund (long-term-care):** **0.58%** of each employee&rsquo;s gross wages (no Social Security cap), **paid
    entirely by the employee** &mdash; employers have **no contribution obligation**; rate is **0.58%** for 2025 and 2026.
    Source: WA Cares Fund &mdash; FAQ (`wacaresfund.wa.gov`).
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** TechBrot configures QuickBooks Payroll for PFML and WA Cares (and federal + FICA + state unemployment),
    with no state income-tax withholding. We set it up and reconcile it; the business or its payroll filer remits.
