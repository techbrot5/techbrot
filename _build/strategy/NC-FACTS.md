## VERIFIED NORTH CAROLINA TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **State individual income tax — a FLAT 3.99% rate (2026), still phasing down.** North Carolina has a **flat individual
  income tax**; for **tax years after 2025 the rate is 3.99%** (down from 4.25% in 2025), and **further reductions are
  possible for 2027 and beyond if revenue triggers are met** (Session Law 2023-134). Frame the rate as **"a flat 3.99%
  for 2026, still on a legislated path downward"** and link the NCDOR rate page. This is the lowest flat rate in our
  Sunbelt set (below Georgia's 4.99%). Source: NCDOR — Tax Rate Schedules; Session Law 2023-134.
- **Corporate income tax — being PHASED OUT TO ZERO (the genuinely NC-distinct headline).** North Carolina is the only
  state actively **eliminating its corporate income tax**. Under S.L. 2021-180 (G.S. 105-130.3C), the rate steps down
  and **reaches 0% by 2030** (no corporate income tax at all). The **2026 rate is 2.00%** (2025 was 2.25%) per the NCDOR
  rate schedule. Frame: **"the corporate income tax is 2.00% for 2026 and is being phased out entirely — to zero by
  2030."** Keep the exact intermediate-year steps QUALITATIVE (link the NCDOR rate page); the **2026 = 2.00%** and the
  **zero-by-2030 endpoint** are the stated facts. Source: NCDOR — Corporate Income and Franchise Tax Rates; S.L.
  2021-180 (G.S. 105-130.3C).
- **Franchise tax — the SURVIVING distinct business tax (the hook; it does NOT phase out).** Even as the corporate
  income tax disappears, North Carolina **keeps its franchise tax** — a net-worth-style annual business tax on
  corporations. Mechanics (VERIFIED, effective tax years beginning Jan 1, 2025):
  - **C corporations:** **$1.50 per $1,000 of the corporation's tax base**, with a **$500 maximum on the first
    $1,000,000** of tax base (i.e., $200 minimum applies; the cap applies to the first $1M, then $1.50/$1,000 above).
  - **S corporations:** **$200 for the first $1,000,000** of tax base, plus **$1.50 per $1,000 above $1,000,000**.
  - **Minimum franchise tax $200**; **holding companies capped at a $150,000 maximum** total franchise tax.
  - The tax base is generally the largest of three bases (net worth, etc.); we keep the equity and balance sheet clean
    and franchise-tax-ready — the CPA computes and files it on the CD-405/CD-401S. Source: NCDOR — Corporate Income and
    Franchise Tax Rates; Franchise Tax.
- **Taxed Pass-Through Entity (Taxed PTE) election — the advisory angle.** North Carolina allows a partnership or S-corp
  to **elect to pay NC income tax at the entity level** (the "Taxed PTE" election, G.S. 105-154.1) as a federal SALT-cap
  workaround. Advisory/coordination item — the CPA makes the election and files (Form D-403 / CD-401S); TechBrot keeps
  the books Taxed-PTE-ready. Source: NCDOR — Taxed Pass-Through Entity.
- **Sales & use tax — 4.75% STATE + local/transit (county-variable; combined 6.75%–7.50%, rising).** The state rate is
  **4.75%**; counties add a **2.00% or 2.25% local rate**, and **four counties add a 0.50% transit rate**, so the
  combined rate runs **6.75% in most (56) counties, 7.00% in many, 7.25% in Mecklenburg and Wake, and 7.50% in Durham
  and Orange**. **Effective July 1, 2026, Mecklenburg County (Charlotte) adds an additional 1%, taking it to 8.25%.**
  Because the rate is set at the county level, QuickBooks must apply the correct combined rate by location. Source:
  NCDOR — Current Sales and Use Tax Rates.
- **NO local/city income tax — and NO income-tax reciprocity.** North Carolina has **no local or municipal income tax**
  (a clean contrast to Ohio's municipal tax and Michigan's city income tax) — payroll income-tax withholding is **state
  only**. North Carolina also has **no reciprocal income-tax agreements with any other state** (like Georgia): a
  nonresident who performs work in NC is generally subject to **NC withholding on the NC-source wages**, which matters
  for multi-state and remote employers. Source: NCDOR — Income Tax Withholding; (NC is among the states with no
  reciprocity agreements).
