## VERIFIED PENNSYLVANIA TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **Personal income tax — flat 3.07%** (in effect since 2004; a single statewide rate, **no brackets**). Applies to
  resident and nonresident individuals, estates, trusts, partnerships, S corporations, and LLCs not taxed as
  corporations. Source: PA DOR Personal Income Tax Rates (`pa.gov/agencies/revenue/.../personal-income-tax-rates`).
- **Local Earned Income Tax (EIT) — the genuinely PA-distinct complexity (Act 32).** On top of the flat 3.07% state
  rate, **municipalities and school districts levy a local Earned Income Tax**, and **each sets its own rate** (rates
  vary widely — commonly around 1% but higher in some places; **do NOT quote a specific municipal rate** — frame
  qualitatively and link the DCED PSD/EIT lookup). Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - **Act 32** consolidated local EIT collection into **Tax Collection Districts (TCDs)** (generally county-based),
    each with a single appointed collector (e.g. Berkheimer, Keystone Collections, Capital Tax Collection Bureau).
  - **PSD codes** (six-digit Political Subdivision codes) identify each employee's **resident** municipality and
    **work-location** municipality; both are needed to determine the correct EIT rate. Employees certify them on a
    **Residency Certification Form**.
  - **"Higher-of" withholding rule:** for a PA-resident employee, the employer withholds at the **higher of** the
    employee's **resident total EIT rate** or the **non-resident rate of the work-location municipality**.
  - Employers with PA worksites **withhold and remit EIT (and the LST, below)**, filing a **single combined
    quarterly return** to the TCD collector, who distributes to the municipalities and school districts.
  - Source: PA DCED Act 32 / Local Income Tax Information (`dced.pa.gov/local-government/local-income-tax-information/`).
- **Local Services Tax (LST)** — a flat local tax on people who work in a municipality that levies it, **up to a
  statutory maximum of $52 per year** where imposed (some municipalities levy less or none). Withheld by the employer
  **pro-rata per pay period at the worksite**; municipalities levying more than $10 must provide a **low-income
  exemption**. Frame the $52 figure as the statutory cap "where levied" and link DCED. Source: PA DCED Local Income
  Tax Information (LST). (Keep municipal specifics qualitative.)
- **Corporate Net Income Tax (CNIT) — 7.49% for 2026**, on a statutory phasedown: **8.99% (2023) · 8.49% (2024) ·
  7.99% (2025) · 7.49% (2026) · 6.99% (2027) · 6.49% (2028) · 5.99% (2029) · 5.49% (2030) · 4.99% (2031+)**. The
  Capital Stock/Foreign Franchise Tax was **eliminated** for tax years beginning Jan 1, 2016 and after. Source: PA
  DOR Corporation Tax Rates (`pa.gov/agencies/revenue/.../corporation-tax-rates`).
- **Sales & use tax — 6% state**, plus **local add-ons in two places only: +1% in Allegheny County (= 7%, Pittsburgh)
  and +2% in Philadelphia (= 8%)**. Everywhere else in PA the rate is a flat 6%. Source: PA DOR Tax Rates
  (`pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-rates`). (Contrast with IN's flat 7%-no-local and DE's 0% — PA is mostly
  6% with two metro add-ons, so QuickBooks sales-tax setup hinges on **where** the sale is sourced.)
- **Realty transfer tax — 1% state** (many municipalities/school districts add a local realty transfer tax on top;
  Philadelphia and Pittsburgh notably higher — frame qualitatively). Source: PA DOR Tax Rates.

## PHILADELPHIA — its own tax world (OUTSIDE Act 32; the City of Philadelphia administers these directly)
Philadelphia is **not** part of the Act 32 EIT system — it runs its own city taxes. Rates change each **July 1**;
**always confirm the current rate at phila.gov** and frame as "current as of the review date."
- **Wage Tax** (on compensation) — effective **July 1, 2025: 3.74% residents · 3.43% non-residents** (down from 3.75%
  / 3.44%). First phase of multi-year reductions through FY2030. Employers must withhold at the new rate on any
  paycheck dated after June 30, 2025. Source: City of Philadelphia Dept of Revenue (phila.gov, 2025-06-18 announcement).
- **Net Profits Tax (NPT)** (on unincorporated business net profits of residents, and Philadelphia-source profits of
  non-residents) — **3.74% residents · 3.43% non-residents** (mirrors the Wage Tax). Credit available against BIRT.
- **Business Income & Receipts Tax (BIRT)** — **net-income portion 5.71%** + **gross-receipts portion 1.410 mills**
  ($1.40 per $1,000 of Philadelphia gross receipts), for tax year 2025 (filed 2026). **The $100,000 gross-receipts
  exclusion was ELIMINATED beginning tax year 2025** (after a court ruling) — **all** businesses with Philadelphia
  activity now file and pay BIRT. Source: City of Philadelphia Dept of Revenue (BIRT) + 2025-06-18 announcement.
- **8% sales tax** in Philadelphia (6% state + 2% local). Pittsburgh/Allegheny **7%** (6% + 1%).
