## VERIFIED ARIZONA TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **State individual income tax — a FLAT 2.5% rate (the lowest flat rate in the country).** Arizona has levied a **flat
  2.5% individual income tax since tax year 2023**, and **2.5% applies for 2026**. It is the **lowest flat income-tax
  rate of any U.S. state**. Frame: **"a flat 2.5% &mdash; the lowest flat income-tax rate in the country."** Source:
  Arizona Dept of Revenue — Individual Income Tax Highlights; 2026 Form 140ES.
- **Corporate income tax — a flat 4.9%.** Arizona's corporate income tax is a **flat 4.9%** of Arizona taxable income.
  Source: Arizona Dept of Revenue — Corporate Income Tax Highlights; Form 120.
- **Pass-Through Entity (PTE) tax — the advisory angle, at 2.5%.** Arizona allows a partnership or S-corp to **elect to
  pay Arizona income tax at the entity level at 2.5%** (matching the individual rate) as a federal SALT-cap workaround.
  Advisory/coordination item — the CPA makes the election and files (Form 165/120S); TechBrot keeps the books PTE-ready.
  Source: Arizona Dept of Revenue — Pass-Through Entity (Form 120/165 PTE).
- **Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT) — the genuinely AZ-distinct tax; the hook (NOT an ordinary sales tax).** Arizona does
  not levy a conventional sales tax. It levies the **Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)** — a tax on the **vendor's
  privilege of doing business** in Arizona, **legally owed by the seller** (who typically passes it through to the
  customer). Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - **State TPT rate is 5.6%**, and the tax is imposed **by business classification** (retail, contracting, restaurant,
    commercial lease, etc.), so the rate and base depend on *what kind of business activity* it is.
  - **Counties and cities add their own TPT on top**, so the **combined rate varies by location and activity**. Most
    **city TPT is now reported and paid through the Arizona Department of Revenue's single centralized portal
    (AZTaxes.gov)**, even though each city sets its own rate (keep specific city rates QUALITATIVE; link the ADOR rate
    table).
  - **Prime contracting classification — the famous AZ construction quirk:** for a **prime contractor**, the TPT base is
    **65% of the gross receipts** from the contract (the other 35% is treated as a standard deduction for materials),
    and the prime contractor — not the subs — generally owes the tax. (MRRA — maintenance, repair, replacement,
    alteration — work is taxed differently, at the point of materials purchase.) This is a genuine, distinctive AZ
    accounting issue for construction.
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** TechBrot configures QuickBooks to charge and track TPT by business classification and location;
    the TPT return is filed by the business or its CPA. Source: Arizona Dept of Revenue — Transaction Privilege Tax; TPT
    Tax Rate Table; Contracting Guidelines / Contracting FAQs.
- **NO local/city income tax — withholding is state-only.** Arizona cities levy **TPT, not income tax** — there is **no
  local or municipal income tax**, so payroll income-tax withholding is **state only** (a clean contrast to Ohio's
  municipal tax and Michigan's city income tax). Source: Arizona Dept of Revenue — Withholding Tax.
- **Nonresident withholding — the 60-day rule + the CA/IN/OR/VA exemption.** Arizona generally **requires withholding on
  a nonresident's Arizona-source wages once the employee has worked in Arizona for 60 days** (with a short-term
  <60-day exception for qualifying employers). **Residents of California, Indiana, Oregon, or Virginia may claim an
  exemption from Arizona withholding** (they file Arizona Form 140NR and take a credit against Arizona tax for tax paid
  to their home state). This matters for multi-state and remote employers. Source: Arizona Dept of Revenue — Withholding
  Exceptions; Form A-4.
