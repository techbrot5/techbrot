## VERIFIED INDIANA TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **Individual (adjusted gross) income tax — flat 2.95% for 2026** (down from 3.00% on Jan 1, 2026), scheduled
  to fall to **2.90% in 2027**. Indiana has a single statewide flat rate (no brackets). Source: Indiana DOR Rates,
  Fees & Penalties + Tax Foundation 2026 Indiana. **A "2.9%/3.05%" figure seen on third-party sites is wrong for
  2026 — use 2.95%.**
- **County Local Income Tax (LIT)** — **all 92 Indiana counties levy a local income tax** on top of the 2.95%
  state rate. **Each county sets its own rate; rates vary by county** (do NOT quote a specific county percentage —
  it is not verified in this file; frame qualitatively and link the DOR county-rate page). Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - The rate is determined by the taxpayer's **county of residence on January 1**. If the taxpayer resides
    out-of-state on Jan 1 but their **principal place of work/business** is an Indiana county on Jan 1, they pay
    that county's LIT rate.
  - **Same rate for residents and nonresidents** — there is no separate resident/nonresident LIT rate; the
    taxpayer pays the full county rate regardless of residency status.
  - **Form WH-4** establishes the employee's county of residence / principal work county for withholding; a new
    WH-4 is required if the employee moves or changes work county during the year.
  - **30-day safe harbor** (IC 6-3-2-27.5, 2024+): compensation of a nonresident employee who works **30 days or
    fewer** in Indiana during the year is exempt.
  - Source: DOR Income Tax Information Bulletin #32 (General Information on Local Income Taxes); Departmental
    Notice #1 (the official, current per-county rate list — `in.gov/dor/files/dn01.pdf`).
- **Corporate (adjusted gross) income tax — flat 4.9%** on Indiana-apportioned AGI. Source: Indiana DOR Corporate
  & Sales Tax History; Tax Foundation 2026.
- **Sales & use tax — flat 7% statewide; NO local sales tax** anywhere in Indiana (no county/city sales tax
  add-ons). Source: Indiana DOR Sales Tax; Tax Foundation 2026. (Contrast with DE's 0% and NY's layered local
  rates — Indiana is a clean single 7% rate, which simplifies QuickBooks sales-tax setup but the **rate still
  applies to taxable goods/services and must be configured and remitted**.)
- **Business tangible personal property tax — exemption threshold raised to $2,000,000 for 2026** (was $80,000),
  per SEA 1 / HEA 1427, effective Jan 1, 2026 (IC 6-1.1-3-7.2): if a taxpayer's total business personal property
  in a county costs **less than $2,000,000** on the assessment date, it is **exempt from taxation** in that county
  — but the exemption must be **declared on Form 102 or 103**. New businesses claiming it must file an initial
  return stating acquisition cost. The PPOP-IN online portal was discontinued beginning 2026. Source: DLGF
  Personal Property; Indiana Form 102/103 (State Forms 10068/11274). **HONEST FRAMING: TechBrot tracks the asset
  detail and prepares/coordinates the Form 102/103 filing posture; it does not file the county return as agent —
  it keeps the books CPA/assessor-ready.**

## GEOGRAPHY
- **92 counties.** Indiana = "The Crossroads of America" — the most interstate-highway-dense state; a national
  logistics/distribution and manufacturing core. Regions: **Central** (Indianapolis metro — Marion + the
  donut/collar counties Hamilton, Hendricks, Johnson, Boone, Hancock, Morgan); **Northeast** (Fort Wayne/Allen);
  **Northwest** ("The Region" — Lake/Porter, part of the **Chicago metro**, Calumet industrial corridor);
  **North-Central** (South Bend/St. Joseph — Michiana); **Southwest** (Evansville/Vanderburgh — Ohio River
  tri-state); **South-Central** (Bloomington/Monroe — Indiana University).

## CITY BRIEFS (8 city pages) — city · county · economy
- **Indianapolis** (Marion County, ~880k city / ~2.1M metro) — **state capital**, largest city & metro;
  **logistics/distribution superhub** (FedEx's second-largest national hub at IND airport, vast warehousing),
  **life sciences** (Eli Lilly HQ, Corteva), insurance & finance, healthcare (IU Health), advanced manufacturing,
  pro sports & conventions, amateur-sports capital. Marion County is consolidated city-county government (Unigov).
- **Fort Wayne** (Allen County, ~270k) — **2nd-largest Indiana city**; advanced/defense manufacturing
  (BAE Systems, Raytheon), insurance legacy (Lincoln Financial roots), healthcare (Parkview, Lutheran),
  distribution/logistics, growing downtown small-business base. Northeast Indiana regional hub.
- **Evansville** (Vanderburgh County, ~115k) — **Southwest Indiana / Ohio River** regional hub for the
  Indiana-Kentucky-Illinois tri-state; manufacturing (Toyota assembly nearby in Gibson County, Berry Global HQ,
  plastics/aluminum), healthcare (Deaconess, Ascension St. Vincent), river logistics, University of Evansville/USI.
- **South Bend** (St. Joseph County, ~103k) — **Michiana** hub on the Michigan line; **University of Notre Dame**;
  post-Studebaker reinvention into healthcare (Beacon), education, tech/startups (Notre Dame's Renaissance
  District / startup scene), advanced manufacturing, RV/marine industry nearby (Elkhart).
- **Carmel** (Hamilton County, ~100k) — **affluent, fast-growing Indianapolis suburb**; dense **corporate-HQ and
  professional-services** base, finance/insurance, tech; nationally known for roundabouts and arts district; high
  household incomes and small-business formation. Hamilton County is one of Indiana's wealthiest, fastest-growing.
- **Fishers** (Hamilton County, ~100k) — **fast-growing Indy suburb and tech hub**; **Launch Fishers / Indiana IoT
  Lab** entrepreneurship ecosystem, IT/software, professional services, life sciences; one of the
  fastest-growing cities in the Midwest. (Shares Hamilton County LIT with Carmel.)
- **Bloomington** (Monroe County, ~80k) — **Indiana University** flagship town; large student/university economy,
  **life sciences & med-device manufacturing** (Catalent, Cook Group nearby in Bloomington/Spencer), research
  commercialization, hospitality, arts. South-Central Indiana hub.
- **Hammond** (Lake County, ~75k) — **Northwest Indiana / "The Region"**, part of the **Chicago metropolitan area**
  (Calumet industrial corridor); heavy industry & steel legacy, oil refining nearby (BP Whiting), logistics/rail,
  small business serving the Chicago-adjacent market; Purdue University Northwest. Lake County's economy and labor
  market are tightly linked to Chicago.
