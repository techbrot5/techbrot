## VERIFIED MARYLAND TAX FACTS (with sources)
- **State individual income tax — GRADUATED, with new 2025 top brackets.** The schedule runs from a low of **2%** up
  to a top marginal rate of **6.50%**. The **Budget Reconciliation and Financing Act (BRFA) of 2025** raised the top
  rate from 5.75% to **6.50%** and added two new high-income brackets: **6.25%** (on the upper band, e.g.
  ~$600,001–$1,200,000 for joint filers) and **6.50%** (over ~$1,200,000); the brackets from 2% up to 5.75% remain
  below those. **Single- and joint-filer thresholds differ** — frame the middle brackets **qualitatively** ("graduated
  from 2% to 6.50%, with new high-income brackets added in 2025") and link the official rate schedule. Source:
  Comptroller of Maryland — Income Tax Rates & Brackets; Maryland General Assembly BRFA 2025.
- **Capital gains surtax (NEW 2025) — 2%** on **net capital gains** for taxpayers with **federal AGI over $300,000**
  (BRFA 2025). *Pending:* 2026-session HB 133 would raise this to 3% and broaden it — **NOT enacted; use 2% as the
  current law and frame any change as proposed.* Source: Comptroller of Maryland; Maryland General Assembly.
- **County "piggyback" Local Income Tax — the genuinely MD-distinct complexity (analog of PA Act 32 / NJ payroll
  stack / IN county LIT).** **All 23 counties and Baltimore City levy a local income tax** on top of the state tax.
  Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - **Each locality sets its own rate**, currently ranging **roughly 2.25% to 3.20%** (do NOT quote a specific
    county rate unless added here — frame qualitatively and link the Comptroller rate list). **Some counties now use
    graduated local brackets** (e.g. Anne Arundel and Frederick adopted income-tiered local rates) rather than a
    single flat rate — another reason to keep it qualitative.
  - The local tax is **collected by the Comptroller on the state return** and is **withheld by the employer based on
    the employee's county (or Baltimore City) of residence**, established on **Form MW507**.
  - QuickBooks Payroll has to apply the correct local rate per employee by county of residence, and the Comptroller
    publishes withholding tables that approximate each local rate.
  - Source: Comptroller of Maryland — Employer Withholding / Local Tax Rates; Maryland Local Income Tax.
- **Reciprocity — MD has agreements with DC, PA, VA, and WV (ties to the PA and VA anchors).** Residents of **DC,
  Pennsylvania, and Virginia** who did not maintain a Maryland abode for **more than 183 days** are **exempt from MD
  withholding** on Maryland wages; **West Virginia** residents are exempt **regardless of time in MD**. The employee
  claims the exemption with **Form MW507 / MW507M**, and the employer stops MD withholding (withholding for the
  residence state instead). Source: Comptroller of Maryland — Employer Withholding; Administrative Release No. 3.
- **Corporate income tax — 8.25%** (flat) on Maryland-apportioned net income. Source: Comptroller of Maryland —
  Corporation Income Tax. (The nonresident-entity withholding rate of 8.25% on MD property sales confirms the rate.)
- **Pass-Through Entity (PTE) tax — the distinct advisory angle.** Maryland allows a pass-through entity (partnership,
  S corp, multi-member LLC) to **elect to pay tax at the entity level** as a SALT-cap workaround, so members may
  deduct the MD tax **federally**; members receive a **credit**. The PTE tax is **7.5% on the distributive share of
  resident/individual members** and **8.25% on nonresident entity members**. Frame as an advisory/coordination item
  (TechBrot keeps the books PTE-ready; the CPA makes the election and files). Source: Comptroller of Maryland —
  Technical Bulletin No. 6 (Taxation of Pass-Through Entities).
- **Sales & use tax — 6% statewide, with NO general local sales tax** — PLUS a **NEW 3% tax on data / information-
  technology / software services, effective July 1, 2025.** Mechanics (VERIFIED):
  - Effective **July 1, 2025**, sales of **data or information-technology services** (2022 NAICS sectors **518, 519,
    or subsector 5415**) and **system/application software publishing services** (NAICS **5132**, which includes
    **business-use SaaS**) are taxed at a **3% rate** (the 3% applies only where no higher rate already applies).
  - **SaaS sold to a business-use customer**, previously exempt under the B2B exemption, is **now taxable at 3%**;
    **SaaS sold to individual end-use consumers continues at the standard 6%** rate.
  - Buyers can use a **Multiple Points of Use (MPU) Certificate** to apportion the tax on digital purchases used
    inside and outside Maryland.
  - **HONEST FRAMING:** this is genuinely new and still being interpreted — TechBrot configures the QuickBooks
    sales-tax items for the 3% IT/data rate vs the 6% standard rate and keeps the books reconciled; **always confirm
    taxability with the Comptroller's guidance (Technical Bulletin TB-56) and your CPA.**
  - Source: Comptroller of Maryland — Technical Bulletin No. 56 (Sales & Use Tax on Data / IT and Software Publishing).
