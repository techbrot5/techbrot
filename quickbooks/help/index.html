    Common QuickBooks issues

    The QuickBooks problems most U.S. businesses run into.

    Not exhaustive. These are the patterns we see repeatedly across our practice — usually because the underlying QuickBooks setup decision was wrong years earlier.

    • Bank feeds stop syncing.

      Bank or credit card feeds disconnect, leaving weeks or months of transactions un-imported. Common with QB Desktop on multi-user. Fix requires manual reconciliation of the gap plus root-cause configuration. Routine cleanup work.

    • Reconciliation discrepancies that won’t resolve.

      Bank reconciliation shows a stubborn variance that previous bookkeepers couldn’t locate — typically caused by deleted-then-restored transactions, duplicate entries, or beginning-balance errors traceable to QuickBooks setup.

    • Multi-state payroll tax configured wrong.

      Employee residing in one state and working in another, or county-level taxes (Indiana CAGIT/COIT, Ohio RITA) misconfigured. Often goes undetected for 6–18 months until a state notice arrives. Payroll ProAdvisor depth catches it.

    • Sales tax not tracking by state.

      QuickBooks shows total sales tax collected but can’t produce state-by-state reports needed for multi-state filings. Configuration issue from initial setup — either fix it once or live with manual filing prep every period.

    • Chart of accounts is unworkable.

      Too many sub-accounts, no logical hierarchy, expenses grouped in ways that don’t produce useful reports, accounts named inconsistently. Typically symptomatic of QuickBooks set up by someone without industry-specific knowledge.

    • Job costing showing wrong numbers.

      Indiana manufacturers, construction contractors, professional services billing project work — job costing produces unreliable margins. Almost always a QuickBooks setup issue: items, classes, and project structures weren’t aligned at the start.

    What it actually looks like

    What QuickBooks help delivers operationally.

    Most “QuickBooks help” offerings are either too narrow (a one-hour consulting call) or too vague (an ongoing “support package” with no defined deliverables). TechBrot’s engagements are scoped as specific operational projects with specific written deliverables.

    For a cleanup engagement, the deliverable is a clean QuickBooks file at year-end: reconciled accounts, fixed chart of accounts, corrected transaction categorization, restored audit trail, and a written summary of what was fixed and why. For a setup engagement, it’s a configured QuickBooks file ready to operate from day one: industry-appropriate chart of accounts, sales tax configured, payroll set up, integrations connected, owner trained. For an migration, it’s a successfully migrated file with reconciled historical data and rebuilt integrations.

    The work is delivered inside your QuickBooks file — we access as accountant-users, you keep your data, the audit trail is visible to you at any time. We never operate in a parallel system or require data export. Books stay your books. We work on them, alongside you, with full transparency.

    For recurring engagements (monthly bookkeeping, ongoing payroll, multi-state sales tax compliance), the deliverable is the same shape every month: bank/credit reconciliation, transaction categorization, financial statements, named-ProAdvisor account ownership, CPA-ready year-end handoff.
