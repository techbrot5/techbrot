    QuickBooks setup, in five questions.

    What is professional QuickBooks setup?

    Professional QuickBooks setup is the implementation engagement where a Certified ProAdvisor configures QuickBooks for a specific business: creating the company file, designing the chart of accounts for the business and industry, entering opening balances, configuring sales tax and payroll, connecting bank and credit card feeds, setting up class and location tracking, and integrating third-party systems. Done correctly, the setup determines whether the books are reliable for the next several years.

    How much does QuickBooks setup cost?

    Fixed one-time engagement, typically $750 for simple single-entity setups, $1,500–$3,000 for standard small business implementations, $3,000–$5,000+ for complex multi-entity, multi-state, or industry-specific work. Diagnostic call produces written scope before work begins. Software subscription costs are separate and pass through at Intuit list pricing.

    Do I need professional setup if QuickBooks has a wizard?

    The QuickBooks setup wizard creates a generic company file with a generic chart of accounts. It does not design accounts for your specific business and industry, does not configure sales tax for your nexus states, does not set up class tracking, does not migrate opening balances, and does not integrate third-party systems. Most setup work TechBrot performs is fixing wizard-driven setups that produced bookkeeping problems in months one through three.

    Which QuickBooks product should I use?

    Product selection is part of the diagnostic. QuickBooks Online Plus fits most U.S. small businesses with inventory, class tracking, or multiple users. QuickBooks Online Advanced fits growing businesses with reporting needs. QuickBooks Enterprise fits larger operations needing advanced inventory or industry-specific features. QuickBooks Desktop Pro and Premier are being phased out by Intuit and not recommended for new setups.

    How long does QuickBooks setup take?

    2 to 4 weeks from kickoff to handoff. Simple single-entity setups complete in 2 weeks. Multi-entity, multi-state, or industry-specific implementations run 3 to 4 weeks. Timeline depends on access to prior records, third-party integration complexity, and client availability for review sessions.

    When professional setup is the right call

    If any of these apply, the answer is yes.

    Setup is a one-time investment that determines years of downstream cost. These are the situations where professional setup pays for itself within months.

    • You’re implementing QuickBooks for the first time.

      New entity, new business, or first-time accounting software. The setup decisions made now determine reporting structure, tax readiness, and audit defensibility for years.

    • You operate in a regulated or specialized industry.

      Construction, real estate, restaurants, professional services, manufacturing, nonprofits, e-commerce — each requires industry-specific chart of accounts and reporting structures. Generic setup creates years of rework.

    • You have inventory, jobs, or class-based segments.

      Inventory accounting, job costing, project tracking, and segment reporting require deliberate setup. Add them later and you lose historical comparability.

    • You collect sales tax in multiple states.

      Multi-state sales tax means multi-state nexus, multi-state filings, and multi-state rate configuration. Sales tax setup is one of the easiest places to get into compliance trouble.

    • You’re migrating from another accounting platform.

      Coming from Wave, FreshBooks, Xero, Bench, or spreadsheets. Migration involves opening balance reconciliation, historical transaction handling, and integration replacement.

    • You’re raising capital or preparing for an audit.

      Investors and auditors read your books. A clean, well-structured QuickBooks file says “professional operator” before any human says it. A wizard-built file says the opposite.

    The setup methodology

    Every setup engagement runs four phases.

    Standards exist so quality doesn’t depend on who’s doing the work. Every TechBrot setup — whether $750 simple or $5,000 complex — follows the same operating sequence.

    1. Week 1

      Setup Diagnostic

      30-minute call. We review your business structure, industry, transaction volume, prior records (if any), integration needs, and reporting requirements. Written fixed-fee scope and implementation plan within 3 business days.

    2. Week 1–2

      File & Chart Build

      Company file created in the right QuickBooks product. Custom chart of accounts designed for your industry. Sales tax, payroll, class and location tracking, and reporting structures configured. Opening balances entered against a defined as-of date.

    3. Week 2–3

      Integration & Data Entry

      Bank and credit card accounts connected. Customer and vendor records entered. Recurring transactions templated. Third-party integrations configured — payment processors, payroll, time tracking, inventory, e-commerce platforms, point-of-sale systems.

    4. Week 3–4

      Verification & Handoff

      First-month transactions reconciled to verify setup is correct. Documented setup summary delivered. Training session with recorded video. Optional transition to monthly bookkeeping with the same operator.

    QuickBooks product selection

    Which QuickBooks product fits your business.

    Product selection is part of the diagnostic, but here’s the shape of how we think about it. QuickBooks Desktop Pro and Premier are being phased out by Intuit and not recommended for new setups.

    • QBO

      Online Simple Start / Essentials

      For sole proprietors and very small operations. Limited reporting and no inventory or class tracking. Most TechBrot setups bypass this tier for QBO Plus.

    • QBO+

      Online Plus

      The default for most U.S. small business setups. Supports inventory, class and location tracking, project tracking, and 5 users. Strongest price-to-capability ratio in the QuickBooks lineup.

    • QBO++

      Online Advanced

      For growing businesses needing custom user roles, batch invoicing, advanced reporting, dedicated account manager. 25 users. Fits the $5M–$50M revenue range.

    • ENT

      QuickBooks Enterprise

      For larger operations needing advanced inventory, industry-specific editions (contractor, manufacturing, nonprofit, retail), or up to 40 users. Hosted Desktop or on-premise.

    • ⚠

      Desktop Pro & Premier

      Being phased out by Intuit. New subscriptions discontinued in 2024; existing licenses lose support by 2027. We migrate Desktop clients to QBO or Enterprise rather than setting up new Desktop files.

    • ?

      Not sure which fits?

      The diagnostic includes product selection. We recommend the product that fits your business — not the one we’d earn more on. Independence is part of the standard.

    What setup is actually for

    Setup is the foundation. The relationship is what comes next.

    The strict technical work of QuickBooks setup — chart of accounts, opening balances, integrations — takes 2 to 4 weeks. The reason it matters runs longer than that.

    A well-built QuickBooks file is the foundation everything else depends on. Monthly bookkeeping runs cleanly because the file was built right. Tax preparation takes hours instead of days because your CPA isn’t fixing structural problems first. Financial advisory work — cash flow forecasting, margin analysis, scenario planning, fractional CFO engagement — only works on top of accurate books.

    Most TechBrot setup engagements continue into monthly bookkeeping with the same operator. Many continue further — into specialized work like multi-state sales tax compliance, payroll management, and as the business grows, the advisory work that distinguishes a profitable operation from a struggling one. The setup is the first decision in a longer relationship. We build the file accordingly.
