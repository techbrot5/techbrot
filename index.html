What’s the difference between accrual and cash bookkeeping?

The key difference lies in when you record your transactions. Cash bookkeeping logs income and expenses when money actually changes hands, which makes it easier to manage day-to-day, especially for very small operations. In contrast, accrual bookkeeping records income and expenses when they’re earned or incurred, giving you:

(i) a more accurate financial picture,

(ii) consistent monthly reports, and

(iii) improved decision-making data.

That’s why techbrot uses accrual accounting from the start—so your books reflect your business’s real performance and you’re always ready to scale.
