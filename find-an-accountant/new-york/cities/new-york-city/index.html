    Bookkeeping built for how NYC actually operates

    Why New York City books are different.

    No U.S. city stacks taxes on a small business the way New York City does — and that, not the software, is what makes NYC bookkeeping its own discipline.

    A Manhattan consulting partnership can face federal tax, New York State tax, NYC personal income tax, and the 4% Unincorporated Business Tax on the same dollar — a layering no other city imposes. A SoHo retailer collects 8.875% sales tax while watching whether their lease crosses the $250,000 Commercial Rent Tax threshold. A Brooklyn restaurant reconciles high-volume POS deposits against that same sales-tax rate. A Queens contractor juggles job costing across boroughs. The bookkeeping has to hold all of it.

    That's why generic, software-only bookkeeping struggles here. When your books don't track the UBT base correctly, you find out via a notice months later. When sales tax isn't reconciled, the quarterly return drifts. When a lease crosses a CRT threshold and no one's watching, it's a surprise liability. TechBrot keeps a named bookkeeper on your file who knows these are the things that matter in this city — and builds them into the monthly close, not the year-end scramble.

    The result: books that reflect how a New York City business actually runs, reconciled every month, and handed to your CPA CPA-ready — so filing the UBT, the sales tax, and the income returns is fast and accurate.

    The three NYC taxes we build into your books

    New York City's tax stack, at a glance.

    8.875%

    Combined sales tax — the highest rate in New York State. Tracked and reconciled in QuickBooks for an accurate quarterly return.

    4%

    Unincorporated Business Tax on sole proprietors, partnerships & LLCs doing business in NYC — applies once gross income tops $95,000; a credit can fully offset it when UBT owed is $3,400 or less. Filed on NYC-202/204 by your CPA.

    3.9%

    Effective Commercial Rent Tax (6% on 65% of base rent) for Manhattan tenants south of 96th St paying $250K+ rent. Small-business credit may exempt under $5M income / $500K rent.
