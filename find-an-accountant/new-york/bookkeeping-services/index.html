    Quick answers

    New York bookkeeping services, in five questions.

    What are New York bookkeeping services?

    New York bookkeeping services are the ongoing recording, reconciling, and reporting of a New York business's finances — bank and card reconciliation, a clean chart of accounts, NYC sales tax tracking, and monthly CPA-ready statements. TechBrot delivers them fixed-fee, with a named bookkeeper on the same file every month.

    What do they cost in New York?

    Monthly bookkeeping runs $400–$2,500+/mo by volume and complexity. If you're behind, a one-time cleanup ($1,500–$15,000+) or catch-up ($2,000–$20,000+) comes first. All fixed-fee, priced in writing before work begins.

    Bookkeeper or accountant — which do I need?

    Most New York businesses need bookkeeping first (clean, current books) and a CPA second (filing, strategy). TechBrot does the bookkeeping and coordinates with your CPA, who files.

    Can you clean up my books first?

    Yes — the standard path is a one-time cleanup to a CPA-ready standard, then ongoing monthly service so the books never drift again.

    Same bookkeeper every month?

    Yes — a named bookkeeper stays on your file, not rotated staff. Continuity is why errors get caught early and the books reflect how your business actually runs.
