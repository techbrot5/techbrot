How to choose the right accountant for your business

Michigan has no shortage of skilled accountants, making it tough to narrow down the best fit. Use these simple steps to find a trusted partner for your business.

  • Step 1: Identify Your Requirements

    Decide whether you need full-service bookkeeping or targeted support, such as preparing financial statements or conducting audits.

  • Step 2: Explore Directories and Read Reviews

    Check local business or tax directories and professional associations. Look at client feedback to gauge reliability. Our QuickBooks ProAdvisor directory is a great starting point.

  • Step 3: Verify Credentials and Experience

    Confirm if the accountant holds recognized certifications such as CPA, CMA, CGMA, or EA, and review their industry experience.

  • Step 4: Connect with Shortlisted Professionals

    Prepare a list of key questions based on your business needs and speak to each candidate to assess compatibility.

  • Step 5: Compare Costs and Finalize

    Evaluate pricing structures and choose the accountant who aligns with both your requirements and budget before onboarding.

Does QuickBooks provide bookkeeping support?

Yes. QuickBooks Live Bookkeeping connects small businesses with experienced virtual bookkeepers who are trained in QuickBooks. Based on your needs, they can guide you through your books or manage them on your behalf.

How can I prepare for a session with a bookkeeper?

For the best results, list your questions in advance so nothing is missed. Choose a time when you can focus without interruptions. It’s ideal to be at your computer with QuickBooks Online open, ready to share your screen if needed.

How do I find an accountant near me?

Start by seeking recommendations from other professionals such as attorneys or financial advisers. You can also explore online resources like the QuickBooks ProAdvisor directory or your state or national accounting associations.

What’s the difference between an accountant and a CPA?

Both manage financial tasks, but CPAs (Certified Public Accountants) meet state-specific requirements and have passed a licensing exam. They can handle advanced services such as audits and tax representation. Accountants may not hold CPA licensure but still provide valuable services like bookkeeping, tax filing, and financial reporting.
