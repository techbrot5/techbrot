QuickBooks Online vs. Xero for growing US businesses.

We run client books on both. Here's where each actually wins — by company profile, not by feature count.

TL;DR

For most US small businesses with payroll, QuickBooks Online wins on ecosystem; Xero wins on price and multi-entity bookkeeping. Past $25M or multi-location inventory, both lose to mid-market systems.

Which is cheaper, QuickBooks Online or Xero?

Xero starts lower ($20 vs $35/mo) and stays lower at comparable tiers.

Which handles US payroll better?

QuickBooks — payroll is native; Xero relies on a third-party integration (Gusto).

Is switching between them hard?

A standard migration runs 2–4 weeks including historical data; we publish fixed migration pricing.

Which do accountants prefer?

In the US, QuickBooks — the ProAdvisor network is far larger, which matters when you need help fast.

What does TechBrot recommend?

Whichever fits your profile — we hold no reseller incentives in either direction.
