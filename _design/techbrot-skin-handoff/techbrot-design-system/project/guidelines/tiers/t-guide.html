What bookkeeping actually costs in 2026.

Hourly, monthly, and fixed-fee rates across the US market — and the pricing structures that quietly cost more than the rate.

TL;DR

US bookkeeping runs $25–$85/hr hourly, $300–$2,500/mo as a service. The rate matters less than the structure: hourly billing rewards slow work, and "starting at" subscriptions reprice after onboarding. Fixed fee with a written scope is the only structure where the incentive points your way.

What does a bookkeeper cost per hour?

$25–$50 for transactional work, $50–$85 for full-charge bookkeeping with month-end close.

What does monthly bookkeeping cost?

$300–$800/mo for low-volume books; $800–$2,500/mo with payroll, inventory, or multi-entity work.

Why do cleanup projects cost more?

Cleanup is forensic work — the price reflects untangling, not entering. Expect $750–$5,000+ by severity.

Is cheap bookkeeping ever worth it?

Only if someone qualified reviews the output monthly. Unreviewed cheap books are the most expensive kind.

What structure should I ask for?

Fixed fee, written scope, named operator. If a provider won't put pricing in writing, keep looking.

The rate is not the price

Every pricing page shows you a rate. None of them show you the structure — and the structure is where the money goes. An hourly bookkeeper at $40 who takes eleven hours costs more than one at $60 who takes six, and you won't know which you have until the close runs long.

The same is true of "starting at $300/mo" subscriptions. The starting price is real; so is the repricing call after onboarding, when the provider has your data and switching feels expensive.

Hourly billing rewards slow work. Subscriptions reward repricing. A written fixed fee is the only structure where the incentive points your way.

What the market actually charges

Across the files we audit, transactional bookkeeping runs $25–$50/hr; full-charge work with a real month-end close runs $50–$85/hr. As a monthly service, clean low-volume books cost $300–$800; payroll, inventory, or multiple entities push engagements to $800–$2,500.
