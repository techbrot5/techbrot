What a forced reconciliation is

When a reconciliation won't balance, QuickBooks offers a way out: an adjustment entry that makes the difference disappear. The button is convenient. The consequence is that your books now assert agreement with the bank without demonstrating it.

The discrepancy didn't go anywhere. It moved into an adjustment account, unlabeled, where it waits for tax season.

A reconciled balance is an opinion with evidence. A forced balance is just an opinion.

TechBrot methodology

Why it compounds

Each month that closes on top of a forced reconciliation inherits its error. By the time a CPA flags it, the original cause — a duplicate deposit, a missed fee, a transposed amount — is buried under months of dependent balances.

The walk-back method

The fix is not to delete the adjustment — that breaks the audit trail. The method is to locate the original discrepancy, document it, and reverse the adjustment with a paired, explained entry. Undo in evidence, not in anger.

Framework

Undo in evidence

Never delete a reconciliation adjustment. Find the source discrepancy, document it, and reverse the adjustment with a paired entry that explains itself — so the trail shows the error and the correction.
